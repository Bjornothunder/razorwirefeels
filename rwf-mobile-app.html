Story

My name is Nicolas Losito. Inside, they call me Redbeard the Great — though the alias has had other forms. It started as a joke. I was teaching myself card tricks in my cell in late 2020, and I thought: every good magician needs a name.

"Out in the real world, I'm a junkie fuckup. In here, apparently I'm one of the smart ones. People come to me for wisdom and insight and fun facts."
— 11/23/20

Starting in 2017, I wrote letters home from inside NCDAC. Those letters became a memoir — Razor Wire Feels — 52 episodes across 5 parts. When they gave us tablets in 2021, the letters became texts. The format changed; the voice didn't.

During the pandemic I used my COVID stimulus check to draw K-Master K & The Hammer: Issue #0, 26 pages. In 2022 I founded Bich Fist Comics as the publishing arm. I also have a son, Mason, who told his mom proudly — "My Dad comes home from prison next year."

FAQ
Is this really free?
Yes. Building your page costs you nothing. There's never a fee to get published here.
Can my family submit for me?
Absolutely. Anyone on the outside can submit on your behalf — just include your name and ID number with the submission.
What types of work can I submit?
Writing (letters, essays, poetry, fiction), artwork, comics — anything you've created. Handwritten or drawn is fine, we'll scan and publish it.
Do I sign over my rights?
No. You keep full ownership. We use an 80/20 publishing agreement — you own the work, we take 20% of any revenue it generates on this platform. Full release form available on request.
Does this platform support people in federal prisons?
Right now we're focused on North Carolina state facilities (NCDAC). We're building toward national coverage — but we have to walk before we run.

About Razor Wire Feels

There are approximately 32,000 people incarcerated in North Carolina state prisons right now. Most of them will never be heard. Not because they have nothing to say — but because no one built them a place to say it.

Razor Wire Feels is that place. A permanent public page for every incarcerated person in NC who wants one. Their writing. Their art. Their music. Their story. Published, preserved, and open to the world.

The platform was founded by Nicolas "Redbeard the Great" Losito, who wrote the original Razor Wire Feels letters from inside NCDAC facilities between 2017 and 2023 — including significant time in solitary confinement. Those letters became the memoir that became the platform.

We are not affiliated with NCDAC or any correctional institution. We are a private publishing platform operated by RWF Publishing LLC.
